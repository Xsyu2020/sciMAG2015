What is sciMAG?

sciMAG is a free and open data set for bibliometrics studies. It has been built by linking the Microsoft Academic Graph (MAG) and the SciMago classification of academic journals

The MAG is made freely accessible on a cloud computing infrastructure and consists of all the academic publication records discoverable on the web dated back to 1870’s. The dataset can be used to conduct research in natural language processing/machine reading, information extraction, entity recognition and disambiguation, machine learning, knowledge discovery, information retrieval, social computing, recommender systems and big data visualization.

The discipline of science of science, that studies the structure and the dynamics of science from publication records, heavily relies on large bibliographic data. Unfortunately the data sets currently available, like the Web of Science (WoS) and Scopus, are proprietary and very expensive. The sciMAG has exceeded the coverage of such data sets, and is totally free. Instead of relying on the original Microsoft classification of paper's research area, we have classified the papers of MAG using SCImago Journal and Country Rank in 2014. There were 21,729 matching journals, which were classified into 306 distinct research topics and 27 unique knowledge areas (a coarser level of classification). The resulting data set has more than 35 million papers. Ongoing research is devoted to show that the sciMAG data set and the WoS are consistent, at least as far as large-scale statistical properties are concerned. We strongly encourage scholars to use this data set in their research.
